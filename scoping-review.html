JBI Methodology

Every review follows the Joanna Briggs Institute framework — the gold standard for scoping reviews. PCC framework, protocol-driven approach, and structured reporting.

📋

PRISMA-ScR Compliant

All deliverables adhere to the PRISMA-ScR (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic reviews and Meta-Analyses extension for Scoping Reviews) 22-item checklist.

🧰

Evidence Mapping

Visual evidence maps showing the volume, nature, and distribution of studies by population, concept, context, and study design. Easy-to-interpret overview at a glance.

Gap Analysis

Systematic identification of evidence gaps, under-researched areas, and methodological limitations in the existing literature to guide future research.

The Process

5 Steps to a Published Scoping Review

A structured, reproducible workflow aligned with JBI guidance — from question to PRISMA-ScR-compliant deliverable.

💬

Research Question

We refine your broad topic into a focused scoping review question using the PCC (Population, Concept, Context) framework.

🔍

Search Strategy

Iterative, peer-reviewed search across PubMed, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science, and grey literature sources.

📰

Screening

Title/abstract and full-text screening by two independent reviewers with conflict resolution. PRISMA flow documented.

📊

Data Charting

Structured extraction of key characteristics and findings into standardized charting tables and evidence matrices.

📄

Report

PRISMA-ScR-compliant manuscript with evidence maps, gap analysis, stakeholder input, and ready-for-submission formatting.
